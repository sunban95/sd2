NAT devices are in wide deployment in home and small office network environment. Due to NAT existence, devices located in different private LANs usually cannot communicate with each other directly.

Smart Connectivity SDK allows app developers to develop remote data sharing and communication applications between remote devices without detailed knowledge of NAT traversal.

Figure 1: Smart Connectivity use case scenario

Figure 1 shows a typical use scenario, two devices are located in different private LANs; both of them have access to Internet via NAT devices. With the help of Smart Connectivity SDK and SC servers on the Internet, they can discover each other, and build reliable or unreliable data connection with each other.

This guide gives introduction to Smart Connectivity SDK concepts and its functionalities, as well as guide to build applications with Smart Connectivity SDK.
